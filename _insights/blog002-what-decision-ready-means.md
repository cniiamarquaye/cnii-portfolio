</p>

<p class="font-semibold text-lg">
What does decision-ready actually look like?
</p>

<!-- ====================================================== -->
<!-- Problem -->
<!-- ====================================================== -->

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">When Technical Output Stops Short</h2>

<p>
Most satellite-derived outputs arrive in a familiar form:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
<li>Classification maps</li>
<li>Vegetation indices</li>
<li>Change detection layers</li>
<li>Time-series trends</li>
</ul>

<p>
They are technically sound. Often rigorous. Sometimes impressive.
</p>

<p class="font-semibold">
But they stop at description.
</p>

<p>
They show what is happening.
</p>

<p>
They do not tell you what to do about it.
</p>

<p class="italic text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">
And for a decision-maker, that gap is everything.
</p>

</div>

<!-- ====================================================== -->
<!-- Gap -->
<!-- ====================================================== -->

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">The Real Question Decision-Makers Ask</h2>

<p>
In technical workflows, the implicit question is:
</p>

<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-slate-200 pl-4 italic text-slate-500">
What does the data show?
</blockquote>

<p>
In operational contexts, the question is different:
</p>

<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-slate-300 pl-4 italic text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">
What does this mean for the decision I need to make?
</blockquote>

<p>
That shift—from observation to implication—is where most outputs fall short.
</p>

</div>

<!-- ====================================================== -->
<!-- Framework -->
<!-- ====================================================== -->

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">A Simple Test for Decision-Readiness</h2>

<p>
In practice, decision-ready insight can be reduced to three questions:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-3">
<li><strong>Where does this matter?</strong></li>
<li><strong>Why does it matter now?</strong></li>
<li><strong>What happens if no action is taken?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>
If an output does not answer these clearly,
</p>

<p class="font-semibold">
it is not finished.
</p>

<p>
Not incorrect. Not useless.
</p>

<p>
Just not yet usable.
</p>

</div>

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">The Difference Framing Makes</h2>

<p>
Consider a typical case:
</p>

<p>
An analysis detects a measurable land use shift or vegetation anomaly.
</p>

<p>
The output includes maps, statistics, and change metrics.
</p>

<p>
All valid.
</p>

<p class="font-semibold">
But none of it drives action.
</p>

<p>
Now compare that with a reframed insight:
</p>

<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-slate-300 pl-4 italic text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">
This area has undergone sustained change over the past six months.  
If the trend continues, it is likely to affect [specific outcome] within [timeframe].  
There is still a window for intervention—but it is narrowing.
</blockquote>

<p>
Same data.
</p>

<p class="font-semibold">
Different outcome.
</p>

</div>

<!-- ====================================================== -->
<!-- Challenge -->
<!-- ====================================================== -->

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">Why This Is Harder Than It Sounds</h2>

<p>
Technical teams are trained to prioritise completeness.
</p>

<p>
More layers. More metrics. More validation.
</p>

<p>
But decision-making does not reward completeness.
</p>

<p class="font-semibold">
It rewards clarity.
</p>

<p>
Getting there requires deliberate choices:
</p>

<ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
<li>Which signal actually matters?</li>
<li>What can be removed without losing meaning?</li>
<li>What consequence is most relevant?</li>
</ul>

<p>
This is not simplification.
</p>

<p>
It is discipline.
</p>

</div>

<!-- ====================================================== -->
<!-- Shift -->
<!-- ====================================================== -->

<div class="space-y-4">

<h2 class="text-xl font-semibold">Moving Beyond Description</h2>

<p>
Environmental intelligence becomes valuable at the point where it influences action.
</p>

<p>
That requires a shift:
</p>

<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-slate-200 pl-4 italic text-slate-500">
Describing change
</blockquote>

<p>to</p>

<blockquote class="border-l-4 border-slate-300 pl-4 italic text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">
Framing consequence and timing
</blockquote>

<p class="font-semibold">
Because decisions are not made on data.
</p>

<p>
They are made on understanding.
</p>

</div>
